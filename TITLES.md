# 🏅 Rank Titles
Titles are premium badges of honor displayed beneath a user's name on their `/rank` card. They are hand-awarded by the Grand Admiral to notable community members. Each title is **unique** and can only be held by one person at a time; assigning a title to a new user will automatically remove it from the previous holder.

### ⚓ The Grand Admiral
The supreme commander of the entire Royal Navy, directing fleet-wide strategy and high-level operations from the Admiralty.

### 🚢 Fleet Commander
An Admiral of the Red, White, or Blue, commanding a specific squadron or an entire regional fleet from their flagship.

### 🎖️ Rear Admiral
A flag officer commanding a division of the fleet, often leading from the rear of the line of battle.

### ⚓ Commodore
A senior Captain appointed to command a small squadron or detachment of ships for a specific mission.

### 👨‍✈️ Ship's Captain
The ultimate authority on the vessel, responsible for the ship itself, its entire crew, and its performance in battle.

### 🗡️ First Lieutenant
The senior-most lieutenant and the Captain's second-in-command, overseeing daily operations and crew discipline.

### 🧭 Sailing Master
A warrant officer responsible for the ship's navigation, pilotage, and the precise handling of its sails and rigging.

### ⚔️ Master at Arms
The ship's chief "police officer," responsible for maintaining order, preventing desertion, and supervising the corporals.

### ⚓ Midshipman
A young officer in training, learning the trade of seamanship and leadership while serving as a bridge between the crew and officers.

### ⚓ Able Seaman
A skilled and experienced mariner capable of "hand, reef, and steer"—performing all the essential duties of working the ship.

### 💣 Master Gunner
A warrant officer in charge of the ship's artillery, gunpowder stores, and the training of the gun crews.

### 🩺 Ship's Surgeon
The ship's doctor, responsible for the health of the crew, managing diseases, and performing surgeries during battle.

### 💰 Purser
The warrant officer responsible for the ship's provisions, clothing, and pay—handling the complex logistics of the voyage.

### 🔉 Boatswain
The warrant officer in charge of the ship's hull, rigging, anchors, and boats, traditionally using a silver whistle to signal orders.

### ⚒️ Ship's Carpenter
A warrant officer responsible for the structural integrity of the ship, repairing damage to the hull, masts, and decks.

### 💥 Cannon Fodder
Those likely to be sacrificed in the heat of battle, often the least experienced or most expendable crew members.

### 🐀 Ship's Rat
A lowly individual lurking where they shouldn't be, nibbling on the ship's biscuits and rations.

### 🏴‍☠️ Mutineer
A crew member who has committed the ultimate naval crime: rising in open rebellion against the Captain's authority.

### 🐚 Barnacle
A persistent nuisance or crew member who is more of a burden than a help, clinging to the ship like a crustacean.

### 🧽 Deck Scrubber
The lowliest duty on the ship, often assigned to those behind on their work or being punished, involving scrubbing the decks with holystone.
